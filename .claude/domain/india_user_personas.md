# India User Personas Model

## Purpose
Provide the system with structured user archetypes relevant to Indian students and young entrepreneurs so recommendations feel grounded instead of generic.

## Persona Set

### Persona A: Tier-2 Student Explorer
- context: undergraduate student from a non-metro city
- likely constraints: limited guidance, affordability pressure, mixed English confidence
- likely needs: clear roadmap, low-cost learning, near-peer mentors, scholarships

### Persona B: Final-Year Career Switcher
- context: student nearing graduation, uncertain about employability
- likely constraints: urgency, confidence gap, lack of portfolio, family pressure
- likely needs: market-fit role targeting, skill-gap clarity, internships, short-term plan

### Persona C: Early Graduate Income Seeker
- context: recent graduate needing near-term earnings
- likely constraints: low runway, need for practical outcomes, possible device or bandwidth limits
- likely needs: accessible job paths, side income routes, affordable skill building, mentor support

### Persona D: Founder-Curious Builder
- context: student or early professional interested in business or freelancing
- likely constraints: capital constraints, limited network, uncertain customer discovery skills
- likely needs: local demand ideas, lean validation, incubator awareness, hybrid employment plus venture path

### Persona E: Resilient But Overloaded Learner
- context: ambitious user balancing study, family obligations, or work
- likely constraints: time scarcity, burnout risk, fragmented attention
- likely needs: sustainable pacing, milestone simplification, check-ins, support accountability

## Usage Rules
- Use personas only as interpretive scaffolding when the prompt lacks detail.
- Never force a user into one persona if the prompt provides stronger facts.
- Expose when a persona assumption influenced the final plan.
